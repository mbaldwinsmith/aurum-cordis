# Aurum Cordis — *The Gold of the Heart*

> *"The Opus Magnum as Christian Way: Alchemy, Theology, and the Soul's Transformation"*

A scholarly, visually rich static site exploring the intersection of medieval alchemical theory, Christian theology, and Jungian depth psychology. Each page traces one stage or concept of the *Opus Magnum* through three lenses simultaneously: classical alchemy, orthodox Christian theology, and Jungian individuation psychology.

---

## Theological Premise

This site does not treat alchemy as a failed proto-chemistry. It treats alchemy as a symbolic language — a *theoria* of matter's hidden intelligibility — that, when read through a Christian lens, illuminates the structure of sanctification, the mystery of the Incarnation, and the soul's journey toward *theosis*.

The interpretive frame is explicitly Trinitarian and orthodox. Where Jung and the alchemical tradition diverge from Christian orthodoxy, this is noted and assessed. Where they converge — especially around shadow integration, the unconscious, and the whole person — the convergence is taken seriously.

The site owes a significant debt to C.G. Jung's alchemical writings (*Psychology and Alchemy*, *Mysterium Coniunctionis*) while insisting that depth psychology is an instrument of pastoral theology, not a replacement for it.
